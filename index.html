William Henry "Bill" Gates III

Software Engineer, Entrepreneur, Philanthropist

Born October 28, 1955. Three children to Melinda French, whom he married in 1994.

Principal founder of Microsoft, the worlds largest PC software company.

Co-founded the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation with his wife, dedicated to philanthropic endeavours.

1968

Learning to Code

At 13 years old, Bill Gates learns BASIC alongside his childhood friend, Paul Allen. More

In the school computer lab - one of the first in the U.S. - they take advantage of bugs in the time scheduler to obtain free computer time. They have their privileges revoked, but are allowed back when they offer to fix the bugs.

They also write a class scheduling program, in which Bill ensures he would share classes with the most interesting girls, and together they start Traf-O-Data, reading traffic data for engineers.

While not considered successful - losing $3,494 in the end - Bill and Allen gain experience simulating microprocessors, which will contribute significantly to their next venture. Less

1975

Leaving Harvard to Start "Micro-Soft"

Allen returns from Washington State University to Massachusetts. He and Bill see a Popular Electronics article featuring the Altair 8800 microcomputer. More

MITS' Altair 8800 is the first successful personal computer. Bill recognises its potential early, knowing it's powerful enough to support an interpreter for BASIC - one of the most popular languages at the time. Racing to beat competition, Bill and Allen call MITS before having started to make their offer, which is accepted.

Using tools from Traf-O-Data, Allen simulates the Altair's chipset while Bill writes 8800 assembly by hand on legal pads. Two other Harvard students - Paul Gilbert and Monte Davidoff - also help with the project.

Despite developing with only a simulated chipset, and Paul finishing the program on the plane to MITS, it works perfectly.

Bill and Allen license the first general purpose programming language for a personal computer to MITS, and start calling their ventures "Micro-Soft". Less

1976

Growing Microsoft

The trade name "Microsoft" is registered with the Secretary of the State of New Mexico on November 26, 1976. More

With the name decided and registered, Microsoft opens its first real offices in One Park Central Tower, Albuquerque, primarily providing BASIC to other computer manufactuers, and hires 4 additional employees.

Bill does payroll, taxes, contracts, marketing, and hiring while developing software himself. Microsoft also starts its first international office in Japan; ASCII Microsoft (now Microsoft Japan). Less

1979

Microsoft Moves to Washington

Microsoft moves its headquarters to Bellevue, Washington, and Bill throws himself into his work. More

With greater access to top programmers, Microsoft grows to 28 employees, each with broad responsibilities for all aspects of the young company.

Microsoft had variants of BASIC for the Commodore 64, original IBM PC, and Apple II. Their first hardware product, the Z-80 SoftCard which allows Apple II to run the Intel CP/M operating system, becomes their primary revenue. Less

1980

MS-DOS and IBM

Bill travels with his mother Mary, a highly respected member of several corporate boards, selling his software. Through Mary, he meets the CEO of IBM. More

Having convinced IBM that he can provide a new operating system for their upcoming PCs without having developed it, Bill purchases exclusive licensing rights of another system, Q-DOS, without telling the developers of his intentions.

Microsoft rebrands Q-DOS as PC-DOS for IBM and retains the rights to ship their own version called MS-DOS, which includes BASIC, and licences it to IBM-clone companies.

At the same time, Microsoft builds their own operating system, Xenix, which becomes the most popular version of Unix by number of machines running it.

IBM and Microsoft's partnership would eventually end with a feud over their competing operating systems. Less

1983

Paul Allen Leaves Microsoft

After a Hodgkin's Lymphoma diagnosis, Paul decides to leave his stressful job at Microsoft. More

Bill and Allen's relationship has become tumultuous. Despite Allen spearheading the deal with IBM that would become an overwhelming success for Microsoft, Bill believes his work on BASIC entitled him to a 60-40 split of the company.

In late December 1982, Paul hears Bill and Steve Ballmer - fellow Harvard student and the first business manager Bill hired at Microsoft - discuss plans to dilute his shares by issuing options to themselves and the other shareholders.

He bursts in on them and shouts, "This is unbelievable! It shows your true character, once and for all." Caught red-handed, Gates and Ballmer are silent.

Allen resigns two months later, keeping his shares in Microsoft that would eventually make him a billionaire. Less

Windows 1.0

The first version of Microsoft Windows is released as a graphical interface on top of MS-DOS. Windows will dominate the PC operating system market for decades.

2002

The Foundation Installs 47,000 Computers in 11,000 Libraries Across the U.S.

Since 1998, The Foundation has committed $250 million to help state libraries continue to provide no-cost access to computers and the Internet for the public as part of the "Staying Connected" program. More

More than 14 million Americans use libraries computer and Internet access, over 2/3rds of which are people with lower income and education levels. Additionally, The Foundation works with Libraries for the Future to strengthen librarians' capacities for community outreach. Less

2006

Warren Buffet Pledges the Bulk of His Wealth to the Foundation

The world's richest person with an estimated worth of $62 billion, Warren Buffet pledges to give the foundation 10 million Berkshire Hathaway Class B shares spread over multiple years, with the first year's contribution being worth around $1.5 billion. More

Buffet sets three conditions; that Bill or Mellinda must be alive and active to the foundation's administration, it must continue as a charity, and each year it must give away an amount equal to the intial gift, plus an additional 5% of its net assets.

This final condition is effectively a matching contribution, doubling the foundation's annual grants. In 2018, Buffet continues his contributions with another $2 billion in Berkshire stock.

TerraPower also launches, with the aims to provide the world with a more affordable, secure, and environmentally friendly form of nuclear energy. Less

2008

Bill Retires from Day-to-Day Duties at Microsoft

Dedicating the majority of his time to the foundation and having recently pledged $50 million to fight the Epola epidemic, Gates moves to the role of chairman and adviser for important projects only.

2010

The Decade of Vaccines

The Foundation pledges $10 billion to research, develop, and deliver vaccines to the world's poorest countries. More

Immunisation is one of the most feasible and cost-effective way of ensuring children survive and thrive in good health. Because of its power and efficacy, renewed efforts are being made globally to immunise all children and develop new vaccines for the remaining killer diseases.

DPT3 is the third and final dose of vaccinations that prevent diphtheria, pertussis (whooping cough), and tetanus - three deadly but preventable diseases. The percentage of children receiving DPT3 is one metric of how well countries are providing immunisation coverage to their children.

The World Health Organisation defined a 90% national DPT3 coverage rate by 2020 as one of the Global Vaccine Action Plan's goals. In 2017, the coverage rate averaged an estimated 88.13%. Less

2011

Reinvent the Toilet Challenge

The Foundation joins global innovators in the Reinvent the Toilet Challenge to support 4.5 billion people who lack safe sanitation. More

The aim of the challenge was to create a toilet that:

  • Sanitises and reclaims energy, water, and nutrients from human waste.
  • Operates without connections to water, sewer, or electrical lines.
  • Costs less than 5 U.S. cents per day to operate.
  • Promotes sustainable and financially profitable sanitation services and businesses that operate in poor, urban settings.
  • Is a trully aspirational next-generation product that everyone will want to use - in both developed and developing nations.

A year later, three winners from the Californian Institute of Technology, Loughborough University, and University of Toronto were recognised for closely matching the required criteria. Less

2013

Efforts to Eradicate Polio by 2018

Over three years, The Gates Foundation gives $300 million to Rotary, joining the Global Polio Eradication Initiative. More

As of November 2018, wild Polio has been eradicated in all but two countries, with 19 cases in Afghanistan and 8 in Pakistan.

With more than 10 billion doses of oral polio vaccine administered to nearly 3 billion children worldwide, 13 million cases of polio have been prevented. Less

2015

The Foundation Offers a Years Paid Leave After Birth or Adoption of a Child

Melinda Gates says, "When we invest in women and children, we are investing in a healthier, more prosperous future for everyone." More

Paid parental leave has been linked to higher birth weights, lower rates of infant mortality, long-term emotional health of both parents and children, and greater engagement from fathers and professional opportunity for mothers. Less

2017

The Goalkeepers Report, Alzheimer's Research, and 'Smart City'

The Foundation launches the first annual Goalkeepers Report - an examination of the progress made in several important areas related to public health including child mortality, malnutrition, and HIV. More

The Foundation identifies infectious and chronic diseases as the two biggest public health concerns to be addressed in the coming decade.

Gates donates $50 million to the Dementia Recovery Fund, and a further $50 million towards start-up Alzheimer's research.

Once of Gate's firms invests $80 million into the development of a proposed city called Belmont near Phoenix, Arizona, with the aims to "create a forward-thinking community with a communication and infrastructure spine that embraces cutting-edge technology."

The city is propsed to contain 3,800 acres of office, commercial, and retail, 470 for public schools, and the rest for 80,000 residential units. Less

2018

Universal Flu Vaccine

Gates awards $12 in grants awarded to individual efforts to develop a universal flu vaccine that are "bold and innovative", indicating more was to come. Clinical trials aim to begin by 2021.
